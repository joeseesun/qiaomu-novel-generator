# Genre Quality Rubric

Use this file during the Intent Hook and Quality Hook. Pick the closest reader promise, then apply the universal checks plus the genre-specific checks.

## Reader Promise

Before writing, name the main reader promise in internal notes:

- 爽文/逆袭: the reader wants visible underestimation, pressure, proof, reversal, and payoff.
- 悬疑/推理: the reader wants a question, clues, false certainty, and an earned reframe.
- 情感/现实: the reader wants a specific human wound, difficult choice, and emotional recognition.
- 修仙/奇幻: the reader wants hierarchy, scarcity, risk, hidden knowledge, and stepwise power change.
- 科幻/概念: the reader wants one clean rule, human cost, rule inversion, and ethical pressure.
- 职场/商业: the reader wants competence under social pressure, real-world stakes, and visible recognition without turning into a lecture.
- 武侠/江湖: the reader wants debt, loyalty, danger, reputation, restraint, and a moral price.

## Universal Rubric

Every story should pass these:

1. A disturbance appears within the first 3 paragraphs.
2. The protagonist wants something visible now.
3. The protagonist has a hidden wound, fear, debt, or value.
4. Conflict escalates through choices, not only accidents.
5. Scenes contain concrete people, objects, places, and actions.
6. Dialogue carries pressure, not explanation.
7. The ending pays off an earlier image, choice, or line.

## Genre Checks

### 爽文/逆袭

- The protagonist is underestimated in a visible arena.
- The protagonist's advantage is seeded before the payoff.
- The payoff happens in public or changes public power.
- The opponent is not only rude; they hold leverage, status, or false certainty.
- The reversal proves value through action, evidence, or outcome, not self-praise.

### 悬疑/推理

- The opening gives a concrete question.
- Each clue can be read innocently at first and differently later.
- The reveal changes the meaning of earlier details.
- The solution is not a random hidden fact.

### 情感/现实

- The conflict is intimate and specific, not a general life lesson.
- At least one scene makes the cost physically visible.
- The ending should feel earned, not merely inspirational.

### 修仙/奇幻

- Power hierarchy, resource scarcity, and risk are clear in scene.
- Hidden knowledge comes from the protagonist's experience or cost, not convenience.
- Treasures, rules, and breakthroughs have limits.
- The final hook widens the world without invalidating the current story.

### 科幻/概念

- Use one main speculative rule.
- Show the rule through ordinary life pressure.
- Avoid lectures about the system.
- The climax should reveal the human cost of the rule.

### 职场/商业

- Competence must be shown through a concrete situation, not explained by resume.
- Avoid turning the story into a process manual.
- Use workplace language only when it creates conflict or proves competence.
- Show a specific user, customer, colleague, money loss, deadline, or public risk.
- Recognition should come from a changed outcome or a reluctant witness.

### 武侠/江湖

- Reputation should matter.
- Dialogue should imply threat, debt, or loyalty.
- Objects can carry history: token, scar, blade, letter, cup, bell, coin.
- A win should leave a moral price or new danger.

## Anti-Exposition Check

If a draft feels like an essay, report, training, retrospective, or product document:

- Replace abstract labels with a person under pressure.
- Replace process explanation with a scene where someone must choose.
- Replace jargon with what the person fears losing.
- Replace "he understood the pain point" with the moment he sees or hears the pain.
- Replace "people recognized him" with a public result, changed decision, or reluctant admission.
